Pani Puri: A Popular Indian Street Food

Overview

Puri: Crispy hollow puris filled with spicy water.

Filling: A mixture of boiled potatoes, chickpeas, and spices.

Pani: Tangy and spicy water made with tamarind and spices.

Preparation

Puri: Deep-fry dough balls to make them crispy.

Filling: Prepare the filling with boiled potatoes, chickpeas, onions, and spices.

Pani: Blend tamarind, mint, coriander, and spices to make the spicy water.

Serving

1. Make a small hole in the puri.

2. Stuff it with the filling.

3. Dip into the spicy pani and enjoy immediately.

Variations

Pani Puri with Ragda: Replace the regular filling with ragda (white peas curry).

Pani Puri Shots: Serve the pani in small glasses with the puris on the side.

Sukha Puri: A dry version with spiced filling and chutneys.

Cultural Significance

Pani Puri is a favorite street food across India, known for its burst of flavors. It is commonly enjoyed as a snack or appetizer and is a must-try for anyone exploring Indian street food.

Special Note: Pani Puri is best enjoyed fresh and immediately after assembling to retain its crispiness and flavor.
