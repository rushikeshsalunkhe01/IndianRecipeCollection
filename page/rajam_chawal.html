Rajma Chawal: A Popular North Indian Dish

Overview

Rajma: Kidney beans cooked in a spiced tomato gravy.

Chawal: Steamed basmati rice served with the rajma.

Accompaniments: Often served with onions, cucumber, and yogurt.

Preparation

Rajma: Kidney beans soaked overnight and then cooked with tomatoes, onions, and spices.

Chawal: Basmati rice washed and cooked until fluffy.

Serving

1. Serve the rajma over a bed of steamed rice.

2. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a dollop of yogurt if desired.

3. Accompany with a side salad of onions and cucumber.

Variations

Punjabi Rajma: Rich and spicy with a thick tomato gravy.

Delhi Rajma: Slightly sweeter and less spicy compared to the Punjabi version.

Cultural Significance

Rajma Chawal is a staple comfort food in North India, cherished for its hearty and fulfilling nature. It is often enjoyed as a family meal and is popular in many Indian homes.

Special Note: Rajma Chawal is best enjoyed hot and freshly prepared for the most authentic taste.
