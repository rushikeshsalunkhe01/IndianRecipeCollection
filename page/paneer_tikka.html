Paneer Tikka: A Popular Indian Appetizer

Overview

Paneer: Indian cottage cheese, cut into cubes.

Marinade: Yogurt, spices, and herbs used to coat paneer.

Grilling: Paneer skewered and grilled until golden brown.

Preparation

Paneer Cubes: Cut paneer into bite-sized cubes.

Marinade: Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and lemon juice.

Marination: Coat paneer cubes with the marinade and let it sit for at least an hour.

Grilling: Skewer the marinated paneer and grill until slightly charred and golden.

Serving

1. Arrange grilled paneer tikka on a platter.

2. Serve hot with mint chutney and lemon wedges.

3. Garnish with sliced onions and coriander leaves.

Variations

Achaari Paneer Tikka: Marinated with pickling spices for a tangy flavor.

Haryali Paneer Tikka: Marinated with a green paste of mint, coriander, and green chilies.

Malai Paneer Tikka: Creamy version with added cream and cheese in the marinade.

Cultural Significance

Paneer Tikka is a popular appetizer in Indian cuisine, often served at parties and gatherings. It is loved for its smoky flavor and spicy marinade, making it a favorite among vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike.

Special Note: Paneer Tikka is not just a dish; it’s a celebration of flavors and tradition. Perfect for any occasion, it’s a must-try for food enthusiasts.
