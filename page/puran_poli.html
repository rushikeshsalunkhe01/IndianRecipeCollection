Puran Poli: A Traditional Indian Sweet

Overview

Puran Poli: A traditional Indian flatbread stuffed with a sweet lentil filling.

Filling: Made from chana dal (split chickpeas), jaggery, and spices.

Accompaniments: Often served with ghee or yogurt.

Preparation

Filling: Cook chana dal with jaggery and spices until it forms a thick, sweet mixture.

Dough: Prepare a dough from flour, water, and a bit of oil or ghee.

Cooking: Roll out the dough, stuff with the filling, and cook on a hot griddle until golden brown.

Serving

1. Roll out the dough into a circle.

2. Place a portion of the sweet filling in the center.

3. Fold the edges over the filling and roll out again.

4. Cook on a griddle until both sides are golden brown.

5. Serve hot with ghee or yogurt.

Variations

With Coconut: Add grated coconut to the filling for extra flavor.

Stuffed with Nuts: Include chopped nuts like almonds or cashews in the filling.

Saffron Puran Poli: Add saffron strands to the filling for a rich aroma and color.

Cultural Significance

Puran Poli is a popular festive sweet in India, especially during festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi and Diwali. It symbolizes prosperity and is enjoyed as a special treat during celebrations.

Special Note: Puran Poli tastes best when served warm, as the filling tends to be softer and more flavorful.
